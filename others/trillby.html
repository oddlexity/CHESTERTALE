The strong breeze and storm served the dusk. It was a morose weather for a decent country, and an unideal time to commit mischief. But, an outstanding murderer differs to defy: he favors the rain to kill his victims, as their teardrops get carried away along their sorrows. They’ll mourn the death of beloved ones, accusing the cause of their demise was because of them, knowing they have met an end at his hands—

“I’ve done it!”

His henchman eyed him. “You didn't go on a spree, did you?”

The stooge is slier and worked based on plans than reckless brawn. His black umbrella covered him. And the alley’s darkness masked his clothes.

“Didn't do, Frisk.”

“I don't believe you.”

He laughed, spinning his dagger. “I target you-know-who.”

“Yeah, yeah.”

The siren’s arrival alerted them. The government attempted to pursue them aplenty, but they always escaped in the blink of an eye. And so they earned the title of top pursued evils.

“They’re here.” —Frisk aligned the umbrella to the center, and raindrops hit his trilby.— “Let’s go.”

He went to his back. “Aye-aye.”

Frisk made a turning motion to the umbrella’s canopies, and they vanished.

A crawling person on the roof saw them fade. They follow them but doesn't come near them.

The Chief came to the alley and caught no one. She gave orders to find their whereabouts. Her instinct is remarkable. Her bullet-sharp eyes catch bitty trails of blood which commoners miss. Even in the rain where liquid proofs are erased. She glanced at the roof in a hunch, looking at nothing. Her subordinate notified the preceptors were nowhere to be seen. She left in irritation. “Tssk, that umbrella punk.”
